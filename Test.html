HelpDesk@IBM Year in Review

Insights and Analysis

Overview

NPS

This page focuses on NPS Scores (2021). It contains analyses and a few recommendations.

In general, IT Support enjoys impressive NPS Scores:

  • 87% of tickets are rated 9 and above.
  • Average NPS across applications is 9.3.
Importantly, ~800k tickets were submitted in 2021 but only ~97k tickets have NPS scores associated.

Incentivizing responses, and critical responses in particular, would likely support more robust analyses of UX.


Summary Statistics:

  • NPS Surveys collect two sorts of data relevant to this review: an NPS Score, and an user-reported Issue Status.

    • Exclusive of Not Entitled tickets, user-reported issue resolution rate is high, at 88%.

  • ~13k respondents completed multiple surveys, suggesting a small subset of highly engaged users.

    • Mean NPS for single respondents and repeat respondents is 9.26 and 9.32 respectively.
    • These data suggest repeat respondents enjoy better UX, on average, than single respondents.
  • Reopen rate, Not Entitled tickets, and Calendar Duration in particular drive user dissatisfaction.

    • For example: the likelihood of an NPS score greater than 8 decreases ~39% when a ticket remains unresovled after 24hrs.

Duration, Application, Issue Status


Duration

Rate of Issue Resolution, and proportion of Promoters, both decrease over time, as depicted in this visual.

More specifically, the odds of receiving a 9 or 10 decrease by 39% after 24hrs. For issue resolution, the odds decrease by ~35%.

In short, the longer a ticket remains open, the more likely UX will diminish. Issues are also more likely to remain unresovled.

Application

NPS varies from application to application. This visual speaks to said variability.

For example, the odds of an NPS score above 8 increase ~1.8x when dealing with 1Password.

Silent Call/Chat has the opposite effect.

Values less than 1 indicate potential areas for improvement, as they lower the odds of a high NPS score, relative to other applications.





Issue Status

Issue Status (user reported) varies from application to application. This visual speaks to said variability.

For example, submitting a Beekeeper ticket decreases the odds of issue resolution 20%, relative to other applications.

Values less than 1 indicate potential areas for improvement, as they lower the odds of issue resolution, relative to other applications.

Recommendations

First Recommendation

Integrate w3 Search data, Slack data, and Service Now data into a single data analytics platform (i.e., create a 'Journey Lake'). Leverage said analytics platform to (1) improve customer journey mapping and (2) develop models predictive of pain points during the journey to issue resolution. In general, greater insight into the customer journey will facilitate a more proactive approach to IT Support.

Second Recommendation

Follow-up with Detractors to ensure adequate issue resolution, to increase visibility into IT Support breakdowns, and to facilitate the discovery of improvement opportunities beyond the scope of those provided in NPS surveys. Liaise with other Technology Support teams (e.g., HRIT, Cirrus) to share best practices and develop a more thorough understanding of pain points driving IBMERs to seek support in general.

Third Recommendation

Implement an heirarchical flagging system like the kind briefly outlined in this analysis. The resultant standardization will support an increasingly automated approach to issue analysis, one that improves visibility into user pain points, and supports real-time responsiveness to high-volume issues. Currently, tickets are flagged consistently at the level of the application, but greater detail requires manual investigation of chat logs and ticket descriptions. A standardized approach to ticket categorization at the level of the issue will improve understanding of deployed applications in terms of their impact on user productivity.

Fourth Recommendation

Develop extractive Question Answering model, and deploy as overlay to IT Support content. Said application will improve self-support, allowing users to pinpoint relevant information effectively and efficiently. Additionally, provide increased support for Not Entitled applications, focusing on documentation in particular. This will ensure users, and chat users in particular, find appropriate channels of support prior to ticket creation. (For example, if users struggle in setting up VS Code, it's not immediately clear how they're to get the right help. The same holds for other common IDEs.)
